Clear fit

Who this DQ200 repair is for and who it is not for.

This page is deliberately narrow. That helps visitors qualify themselves fast and stops the page from trying to rank for every DSG problem under the sun.

This page is for you if

  • The DQ200 TCU is completely silent or unreachable by diagnostics
  • Other modules log lost communication with the TCM
  • PRNDS flashes and the controller appears offline
  • The 30A mechatronic fuse blows when the TCU is connected
  • You have a 0AM or 0CW unit from a VW, Audi, Skoda or SEAT vehicle
  • You want your original module repaired and returned

Message first if your main fault is

  • P189C or P17BF relating to hydraulic pressure or accumulator faults
  • Odd-gear or even-gear drive loss with normal TCU communication still present
  • Pure clutch, selector or gearbox mechanical faults
  • Water damage, severe corrosion or heavy physical case damage
Not every DQ200 fault is a no communication repair.

Symptoms and fault codes

Common symptoms and DTCs seen on genuine DQ200 no communication jobs.

These are the typical symptoms and codes customers report before sending the TCU in.

Common symptoms we see

  • No communication with TCU or cannot connect via diagnostics
  • Intermittent communication drop-outs followed by full silence
  • PRNDS flashing, limp mode or no drive with TCM offline logs elsewhere
  • 30A mechatronic fuse blowing when the TCU is plugged in
  • Gear indicator missing, blank or gearbox warning displayed
  • Power, ground or CAN concerns that trace back to the controller

FAQ

Questions customers ask before sending a DQ200 unit.

Straight answers before you pack the box.

Does this repair fix all DQ200 faults?

No. This page is specifically for genuine no communication faults on DQ200 0AM and 0CW TCU units. Pressure, accumulator, clutch and mechanical gearbox issues are a different path.

Does this fix no communication faults?

Yes. This service is for units that are silent or cannot be reached by diagnostics. We repair the communication path where viable and verify the result on the bench.

Do I need coding after refitting?

Refitting your original repaired TCU usually requires no coding. Clear the faults and perform DSG basic settings or adaptations if prompted by the vehicle.

What is the turnaround time?

Standard turnaround is usually 1 to 2 working days from receipt. Express handling aims for same-day or next-day work where the parcel arrives before 11am and the unit is suitable for immediate repair.

What happens if the unit is not repairable?

If there is heavy corrosion, severe PCB damage or another unrelated internal fault path, we will advise before proceeding. The diagnostic-only fee is £45 and is waived if the unit is repaired.

Can I bring the complete vehicle?

Yes, by appointment. On-car remove, repair and refit work starts from £449 plus oil or gaskets where required.

What should I include in the parcel?

Include your name, phone number, return address, registration or VIN, part number, fault codes if known, and a pre-paid return label inside the box.
