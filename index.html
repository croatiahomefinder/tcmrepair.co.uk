Step-by-step

Exactly how the send-in process works

Clear process removes hesitation. The customer needs to know exactly what to do before shipping, and exactly what happens after the unit arrives at the workshop.

What you do before shipping

Customer steps

1

Remove and clean the TCM

Separate the transmission control module from the mechatronic unit and give the module a sensible clean before packing.

2

Pack it securely

Wrap the TCM properly in bubble wrap and place it inside a sturdy box so the unit cannot move around in transit.

3

Include your details

Put your full contact details, vehicle make and model, and any fault description or DTC codes inside the box.

4

Include a prepaid return label

You must include a prepaid return shipping label in the box so the repaired unit can be sent straight back to you once complete.

5

Choose your courier and post it

You choose the courier, you control the shipment, and you remain responsible for courier-related risk both ways.

What we do once it arrives

Workshop steps

1

Receive and test

The unit is booked in, inspected and tested properly to confirm the fault condition.

2

Repair

The identified internal issue is repaired using our specialist process. The method stays in the workshop, not on the website.

3

Send diagnostic and repair report

You receive a clear outcome report so you know what was found and what has been done before payment is requested.

4

Send secure payment link

Once the repair is verified, a secure payment link is sent for Stripe or PayPal settlement.

5

Return via your prepaid label

The unit is packaged and returned promptly using the prepaid label you included in the box.
